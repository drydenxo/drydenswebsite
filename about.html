About Me


My name is Dryden (he/him) and I'm a student studying Library & Information Tech at Algonquin College in Ottawa! While learning to become a library technician is my top priority at the moment, there are other things I'm equally passionate about. Two of these passions are for music, and for my Canadian identity. This website is a creative outlet for me to combine these two interests in a fun way. Something that personally bothers me in the Canadian media landscape is how oversaturated American content is. It's unavoidable, but I find oftentimes our own Canadian content get buried in the mix. One area I especially find this in is music. I listen to music daily; it's one of my favourite things to do. However, there was a time where I knew very little about Canadian music, because most of what I consumed was American.


My shift away from The US is also largely motivated by the country's political trajectory since their election in 2024. Rhetoric about Canada needing to be annexed and become the "51st state" has enkindled a sort of militant patriotism, as well as the "buy Canadian" movement, across Canada as forms of protest. In a way, I think this was exactly what I needed to have happen, because I feel more in touch with my Canadian roots than ever before. Up until last year, I had never been this dedicated to supporting Canadian artistry; but now, I feel it is very much shaping who I am and it is a crucial part of how I would define myself.
